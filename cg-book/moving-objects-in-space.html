moving objects in space

Suppose we have a frame with origin point $O$ and two vectors $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ pointing in the $x$ and $y$ direction respectively. Then we can say that

$$P = x\vec{u} + y\vec{v} + O$$

which means that we can get to any point inside the frame by adding together scaled versions of the vectors $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$, resulting in a new vector and adding that to the origin $O$, resulting in a new point $P$.

We can also write this using matrices

$$P = \begin{bmatrix}x & y & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}\vec{u}\\\vec{v}\\O\end{bmatrix}$$

which is a common trick that we use.

It might be confusing to see a two-dimensional point written as a vector with three elements. Or rather, why is that $1$ in there? Well if you look at the first definition of $P$ above, you will see that we also need to pick up the origin point $O$ and having a one in the row matrix makes sure we will pick up the $O$ that appears in the column matrix when multiplying them together.

translation

We use translation to translate a point $\left(x, y\right)$ to a position $\left(x+a, y+b\right)$.

$$\begin{bmatrix}x & y & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ a & b & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}x+a & y+b & 1\end{bmatrix}$$
